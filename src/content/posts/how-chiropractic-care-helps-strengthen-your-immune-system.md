How often you fight off seasonal bugs has less to do with luck and more to do with how well your body is communicating with itself.

Your **nervous system** is the master control center for every function in your body, including your **immune system**. When spinal segments aren't moving well, that mechanical stress can affect how nerves carry signals between the brain and the rest of the body. That's where **chiropractic care** comes in.

## The Science Behind It

Research shows chiropractic adjustments can directly support immune health:

- 🧠 **Improved communication:** Adjustments may help support nerve flow between the brain and immune organs, so your body can detect and respond to stressors more efficiently.

- 💪 **Immune cell activity:** Early research in _The Journal of Manipulative and Physiological Therapeutics_ (Brennan et al.) reported short-term changes in immune cell activity following spinal manipulation. The findings are promising but preliminary, and chiropractic care should not be considered a substitute for vaccinations or other infectious-disease prevention.

- 😌 **Reduced stress hormones:** Some studies suggest chiropractic care may help modulate cortisol — a stress hormone linked to immune suppression — supporting the body's stress-recovery response.

- 🔄 **Less inflammation:** Emerging research suggests adjustments may influence inflammatory cytokine signaling, which is one factor in how the immune system regulates itself.

## The Big Picture

When your spine is aligned and your nervous system is clear, your immune system can work smarter — not harder. Regular chiropractic care keeps your body's communication pathways open, allowing you to **adapt, heal, and defend naturally**.
